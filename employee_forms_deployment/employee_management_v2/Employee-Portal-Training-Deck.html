What Is the Employee Portal?

A Google Apps Script system that automates paperwork across every employee lifecycle event.

Replaces
  • Manual emails and text chains between managers and HR/IT
  • Untracked spreadsheet entries and verbal handoffs
How It Works
  • ~50 managers submit requests through guided web forms
  • Each step auto-notifies the right person once the previous step is done
  • The system tracks every open item — nothing falls through
Four Supported Events
  • New employee joining the company
  • Existing employee needs additional access or equipment
  • Employee leaving (End of Employment)
  • Employee changing role, site, manager, or classification
How It Works: Sequential Gating

Each step is locked until the previous actor completes their task.

The Chain
  1. 1.Manager submits a request — ticket created in the system
  2. 2.First actor gets an email with a direct link to their action form
  3. 3.They complete their step — system records the result
  4. 4.Next actor is notified automatically — chain continues
  5. 5.Ticket closes when all steps are done
For Managers
  • No follow-up emails needed — the system handles all notifications
  • Dashboard shows exactly which step is waiting if something stalls
  • Routing is automatic (hourly vs salary, access vs no access)
  • Every step is timestamped and auditable
The Four Workflows

New Employee Request
Hiring a new employee (hourly or salary). Covers ID setup, HR verification, IT access, specialist tasks, and 30/60/90 review.
Equipment and Systems Request
Existing employee needs additional access or equipment. No HR gate — goes straight to IT Confirmation then IT Setup.
End of Employment (EOE)
Employee leaving the company. HR approval gate for terminations. System access removal, Google offboarding, equipment return.
Status and Position Change
Role, site, manager, or classification change. HR approval required. New access provisioned and old access removed in one request.
New Employee Request — Overview

Submitted by: Manager or supervisor  ·  Before or on the employee’s first day

Routing at Submission
  • Salary? Triggers JR title assignment and 30/60/90 review
  • System / equipment access needed? Triggers IT Confirmation gate
  • No access needed? Skips IT steps entirely
Steps
  1. 1.ID Setup — Employee ID, SiteDocs Worker, DSS Training account
  2. 2.Safety Onboarding — SiteDocs locations and DSS learning paths
  3. 3.HR Verification — Cross-check name, title, manager, ADP ID
  4. 4.IT Confirmation — Reviews system access scope (if access requested)
  5. 5.IT Setup — Google account, computer, phone, BOSS provisioned
  6. 6.Specialists — Credit card, business cards, Fleetio, Jonas (parallel)
  7. 7.30/60/90 Review — Salary employees only (parallel)
Equipment and Systems Request — Overview

Submitted by: Manager of an existing employee  ·  Any time access or equipment is needed

Use This Form When
  • Employee moves to a new role and needs BOSS access for new committees
  • Existing employee needs a Google account for the first time
  • Equipment is being issued to a current employee
Steps
  1. 1.IT Confirmation — Reviews and approves the access scope
  2. 2.IT Setup — Google account, computer, BOSS, systems provisioned
  3. 3.Specialists — Credit card, Jonas, Fleetio, business cards (parallel)

No HR gate  ·  No ID Setup or Safety step  ·  Fastest path for access changes

End of Employment (EOE) — Overview

Submitted by: Manager of the departing employee  ·  As soon as the departure is known

Departure Types
  • Resignation / Retirement — No HR approval, proceeds to offboarding directly
  • Termination — HR must approve before any offboarding steps begin
Captured at Submission
  • Last day worked and final pay date
  • Systems to remove access from (ADP, BOSS, Google, Jonas, SiteDocs…)
  • Google offboarding options (Drive transfer, email forwarding)
  • Equipment to be returned — computer, phone, tablet, vehicle, card
  • Direct reports and whether reassignment is needed
Steps
  1. 1.HR Approval (terminations only) — approves or rejects with notes
  2. 2.IT Offboarding — access removal, Google suspension or transfer
  3. 3.Equipment Collection — items tracked and confirmed returned
Status and Position Change — Overview

Submitted by: Manager of the changing employee  ·  Before the change takes effect

Change Types (check all that apply)
  • Site Transfer — new site and department sub-section appears
  • Position Change — new job title and JR title fields appear
  • Classification Change — hourly-to-salary toggle with supporting details
  • Manager Change — new manager email field appears
Also Captured in One Request
  • New system / equipment access to provision
  • Equipment to return — computer, phone, tablet, vehicle, card
  • Access to remove (ADP, BOSS, CAA, Google, Jonas, SiteDocs…)
  • Handling of any direct reports
Steps
  1. 1.HR Approval — reviews all changes, confirms new title and JR title
  2. 2.IT + Specialists — new access provisioned, old access revoked (parallel)
